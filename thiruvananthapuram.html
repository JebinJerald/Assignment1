Thiruvananthapuram


Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city of Kerala, blushes in regal splendour. Such is the historic legacy that the moment you enter this district, you are transported to the age of the kings and the conquerors. With ancient palaces, royal complexes, museums and forts, Thiruvananthapuram bathes in the glory of the yesteryears. This city has everything a traveller needs. You can either walk along the royal corridors and experience the royal charm or tread through the rich natural bounties of nature. Endowed with a wide variety of flora and fauna, wildlife, beaches, mountains and backwaters, one would never be short of any activity to indulge in while being here. To be in the royal city is to be in a space and time that serenades one into a state of bliss and contentment. Life flows at a surreal pace here.

Top Spots


  • Kovalam

    • A beach lover's paradise, Kovalam is a small coastal town in Thiruvananthapuram, the capital of Kerala. With its three crescent-shaped beaches and the cultural, heritage, wellness, zen and adventure sports experiences, Kovalam is the ideal destination to enjoy one's vacation.

    Kovalam is formed of three beaches viz. the Hawa Beach or Eve's beach; Grove's Beach (Samudra Beach), which is also called Kovalam Beach, and the Light House Beach. The lighthouse beach is famed for its towering lighthouse which offers the best vantage point view of the coastline in southern Kerala.

    The range of experiences includes sunbathing, surfing, swimming, catamaran cruise, boating, scuba diving, parasailing along with getting in touch with one's inner self by indulging in yoga or Ayurveda therapy. You can also visit the fishing hamlets to experience local life.


  • Veli Tourist Village

    • At the Veli Tourist Village, one gets to hop onto a mini-train modelled in the form of a vintage steam engine, experience the steam gushing out of the toy train as one enjoys the joy ride in the scenic surrounds of Veli. As you trundle along the lush surrounds of Veli, feel the wind in your face and meet the child in you.

    There is never a dull moment here with it being one of the go-to picnic spots in the city. Be it lazing away on the golden sands and watching the sun dip into the horizon or pedaling through the placid waters of the lake, Veli Tourist Village has everything to satiate the wanderlust in you.

    The village lies where the Veli Lake meets the Arabian Sea. Marvel at the sculptures or, better, indulge in the many recreational activities on offer at the tourist village. Plenty of boating opportunities await travellers here. The well-equipped Children's Park offers plenty of exciting rides for kids. Alternately, one can enjoy the sights and sounds of Veli ensconced in the mini train as it chugs through the village. And finally, wind up your journey at the floating restaurant and enjoy your meal, all buoyed up in the quiet waters of the lake. The Veli Tourist Village is indeed your ideal escape from the humdrum of the city.


  • Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

    • The wealthiest temple in the whole world, where gemstones, coins and other treasures to the tune of trillions of dollars lie in the vaults. One of the ancient temples in the state, the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple which blends the Chera and Dravidian styles of architecture evokes a sense of wonderment among anyone with a penchant for history and lores. The temple rose to fame in the global media after the wealth was discovered in its secret vaults. The treasure has been inventoried and still remains secured in its vaults, hidden from public view.

    Located inside the magnificent fort complex in Thiruvananthapuram, the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple's history dates back to the 8th century and is one of the 108 sacred Vishnu temples in India. The presiding deity of this temple is Lord Vishnu, reclining on Anantha, the hooded Serpent.

    The chamber where the presiding deity is kept is an architectural marvel. Devotees throng the area near the sanctum sanctorum to catch a glimpse of their deity. The idol is about 18 ft long and can be viewed through three different doors. While the head and chest are seen through the first door; the hands can be observed through the second and the feet through the third. The aesthetics and the architecture behind this are often lauded by visitors. Beautiful paintings, intricate wooden work and murals adorn the interiors of the temple. The heavily ornate structures in the temple speak volumes about the stellar art and craftsmanship in the olden days.


  • Poovar

    • An unspoilt green labyrinth ready to be explored endlessly - that's what Poovar is for you. A picturesque, secluded spot with gorgeous backwater stretches, Poovar is a small coastal village in Thiruvananthapuram that offers plenty of recreational activities. As you cruise across the gentle backwaters in a houseboat, be awed by the tranquil pace of life at the village or focus on the birds of varied plumage vying for your attention at the mangroves. Or better still, laze away in the canoe and let the waters rock you as you watch the sun take a dip in the horizon

    Once here, the rewards are manifold for a traveller. You get to experience the Poovar Lake, Neyyar River, Arabian Sea and the golden sandy beach in all its glory. A haven of biodiversity, Poovar is also a much sought-after locale by birders for birdwatching.

    Sunrise and sunsets at Poovar are quite famous and the best way to experience them is by drifting across the azure blue waters. Plenty of boating options are available for the traveller here and if one needs to try their hands at traditional fishing techniques or other indigenous art and craft making, one needs to just seek the help of a local village person. If you are a beach bum, you can opt to explore the sandy beach which is known for its serene, solitary stretch.


  • Neyyar Dam and Park

    • Be it gliding across the waters of Neyyar watching the 'wild life' unfold on the shores, or lazing away in the park and experiencing moments of tranquillity or merging with the lush surrounds and staying still as you watch the mighty king of the jungle, the Neyyar Dam and Park offers an experience that can leave an indelible mark on any traveller.

    Here, you experience the wilderness like no other. The Neyyar Dam is a picturesque picnic spot that is frequented by locals and travellers alike. It is part of the Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, a verdant oasis, which boasts of a lush 12,000 hectares of natural vegetation. The rich flora and fauna of the sanctuary along with the many trekking trails is any nature enthusiast's dream come true.

    The Watch Tower, Deer Park, Lion Safari Park and Crocodile Rehabilitation and Research Centre at Neyyar offer a wholesome travel experience replete with fun and blissful moments. At the reservoir, one may avail of the boating facilities where you can sail through the gorgeous waters of Neyyar lake and experience the panoramic view of the area.
